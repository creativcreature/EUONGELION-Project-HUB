Direction One

Sacred Minimalism

"Museum catalog meets illuminated manuscript"

Clean geometric forms floating in generous negative space. Subtle gold leaf textures catching light like ancient icons. A limited palette that feels both contemporary and eternal. This direction says: "The Gospel is sophisticated. It belongs in the Tate Modern AND the catacombs."

Form

Geometric abstraction over literal imagery. Shapes that suggest rather than depict. The sacred geometry of creation.

Color

Limited palette ‚Äî 3-4 colors maximum. Tehom black, scroll white, gold as sacred accent. Restraint as reverence.

Typography

Type as architecture. Confident sans-serif with generous tracking. Hebrew isolated and elevated.

Space

Extreme negative space. Sabbath rest in visual form. Let the Word breathe. Less is holy.

Texture

Subtle gold leaf fragments. Paper grain. The tactile memory of illuminated manuscripts.

Mood

Kinfolk magazine if it found Jesus. A gallery exhibition catalog. Quiet confidence.

Holy Grit

"Revival tent meets independent press"

Risograph textures. Screen-print imperfection. The feeling of finding religious pamphlets in your grandmother's drawer ‚Äî but designed by someone who worked at Criterion Collection. This direction says: "Faith is tactile. It gets its hands dirty. It has texture you can feel."

Texture

Visible grain. Halftone dots. Paper fiber. Every surface has memory. The patina of use.

Color

Limited spot colors ‚Äî 2-3 inks like risograph. Overprint effects. Intentional registration errors.

Typography

Bold. Condensed. Hand-drawn lettering. Letterpress impressions. Type that's been through something.

Imperfection

Ink bleeds. Worn edges. The beauty of the handmade. Perfection isn't the goal ‚Äî truth is.

Composition

Poster-ready. Bold, immediate. Designed to be wheat-pasted on a wall in the village square.

Mood

A24 film poster meets old Sunday school materials. Indie rock aesthetics meet ancient truth.

Direction Three

The Illustrated Gospel

"New Yorker meets Sunday school"

Editorial illustration that tells stories. Clever use of negative space. Simple shapes that carry enormous emotional weight. The kind of images that stop you mid-scroll and make you think. This direction says: "Scripture is story. Let's illustrate it like the narrative masterpiece it is."

Visual Moodboard

Olly Moss

Narrative

Every image tells a story. Visual parables. The illustration is the first sentence of a sermon.

Negative Space

What's not shown matters as much as what is. Clever reveals. The viewer completes the image.

Simplicity

Reduced shapes. Flat color. Complexity comes from concept, not rendering. A child could draw it, but didn't.

Emotion

Images that make you feel something. Not decoration ‚Äî communication. Every stroke has purpose.

Wit

Visual cleverness. Double meanings. The "aha" moment. Illustrations that reward attention.

Mood

McSweeney's meets Ecclesiastes. Olly Moss movie posters for parables. Thoughtful, not precious.
