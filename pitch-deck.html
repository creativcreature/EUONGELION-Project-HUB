01

Sacred Minimalism

"Museum catalog meets illuminated manuscript"

The Vision

Clean geometric forms floating in generous negative space. Subtle gold leaf textures catching light like ancient icons. A limited palette — tehom black, scroll white, touches of gold — that feels both contemporary and eternal.

This direction says: "The Gospel is sophisticated. It belongs in the Tate Modern AND the catacombs."

Think exhibition catalog for a show about faith. Think Kinfolk magazine if it found Jesus. Think the quiet confidence of a monastery library.

Artist DNA

ISO50 / Scott Hansen Tom Gauld Makoto Fujimura
  • Geometric abstraction over literal imagery
  • Limited palette (3-4 colors max)
  • Typography as art, not afterthought
  • Gold leaf textures as sacred accent
  • Extreme negative space — let the Word breathe
02

Holy Grit

"Revival tent meets independent press"

The Vision

Risograph textures. Screen-print imperfection. The feeling of finding a stack of religious pamphlets in your grandmother's drawer — but designed by someone who worked at Criterion Collection.

This direction says: "Faith is tactile. It gets its hands dirty. It has texture you can feel."

Think A24 film poster meets old Sunday school materials. Think the beautiful imperfection of letterpress. Think what happens when indie rock aesthetics meet ancient truth.

Artist DNA

True Grit Texture Aesthetic Apparatus Hato Press DKNG Studios
  • Visible texture — grain, halftone, paper
  • Limited ink colors (2-3 spot colors)
  • Intentional imperfection
  • Bold, poster-ready compositions
  • Feels printed, not rendered
03

The Illustrated Gospel

"New Yorker meets Sunday school"

The Vision

Editorial illustration that tells stories. Clever use of negative space. Simple shapes that carry enormous emotional weight. The kind of images that stop you mid-scroll and make you think.

This direction says: "Scripture is story. Let's illustrate it like the narrative masterpiece it is."

Think Olly Moss movie posters for parables. Think Brian Rea's emotional line work for the Psalms. Think what McSweeney's would do with Ecclesiastes.

Artist DNA

Brian Rea Olly Moss Geoff McFetridge Tom Gauld
  • Narrative-driven imagery
  • Clever negative space concepts
  • Simple shapes, complex meaning
  • Emotional resonance over decoration
  • Each image tells a micro-story

My Recommendation

Don't pick one. Layer them.

Sacred Minimalism as the foundation — the overall system.
Holy Grit for texture and warmth — the tactile layer.
Illustrated Gospel for key moments — the storytelling beats.

"A visual language as layered as Scripture itself."
